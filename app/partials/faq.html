Goat Questions? We goat answers!

You'll find answers to most common questions about the co-op here. Got a question that's not covered? Please contact us.



You can sign up as a Producer or Customer member here. You will then be required to pay the invoiced amount.

Please note that if you want to become a Producer Member, you will need to fill out a specialised application form, which will be reviewed before your membership can be approved. However, anyone can sign up as a Customer member.

Becoming a Producer Member allows you to use our website to sell your products. The slightly higher cost allows us to continue maintaining the website for you, and pays for reveiwing and approving Producer applications.

Most producers recoup the cost of membership quickly by selling their products through the NNFC. However, should a producer wish to leave the co-op, the membership fee is completely refundable.

Payment deadlines are marked on our calendar. If we have not received your payment by this date, we will send you a reminder.

Why only have a set time?

A set time for uploading is used to seperate uploading from ordering. It's analogous to the time spent setting up your booth at a farmer's market. If people tried to setup while customers were browsing for products, slow producers might miss out on customers and customers might miss the best deals.

In most cases, if you grew it, made it, or raised it in Northland, you can sell it. However, there are a few exceptions.

We are proud to be GE free. Genetically engineered products will not be accepted for sale. This extends to goods that have GE ingredients, and animal products where the animal was fed genetically engineered material. As a Natural Food Co-op, we also require that producers declare all chemicals used in their production process. You can still sell your goods through the NNFC, but all chemical agents must be declared.

Finally, for the sake of the animals, we do not accept goods derived from Confined Animal Feeding Operations (such as battery hens). Please keep your livestock free range.

Check the calendar on our website to find out what days you can shop. All memebers will receive emails notifying them that ordering is now open.

No webpages are restricted outside of shopping time but there will be no products in the store until the producers begin uploading products again.

Why only have a set time?

A set time for shopping is used because the co-op is technically not a store. We have no inventory and no way to ship orders on our own. We do show you what other producers are selling and facilitate a convenient and efficient distribution system via volunteer sorters and drivers.

Check the calendar on our website to find out when you must have paid your shopping invoice to us. All memebers who our records have not confirmed as having paid will receive a reminder on this day.

Why can't I pay by credit card?

There is a large number of reasons we don't accept credit cards. The main one being we are trying to keep costs down for the time being. If you'd like a feature for paying directly online please give us that feedback in an email.

Member's orders are collected and sorted on Delivery Day. Delivery day is generally the last Wednesday of every month.

If you live in Whangarei, or want to collect your food while you’re in town, you can pick up your order from The Woods (3 Woods Road), in central Whangarei. Orders are avaible for collection from 3:00 p.m. onwards on Delivery Day. You can find out when the next Delivery Day is by checking the calendar on our wesite.

If Whangarei is too far for you to travel, you can pick up your goods from a collection point closer to home. To find out times and places for community collection points, you’ll need to join a delivery route. Please also get in touch with your local Route Manager. Contact details for our Route Managers in communities all over Northland can be found here.

Producers should bring the goods their customers have ordered to The Woods, our sorting facility in Central Whangarei. You should also bring a copy of your order list with you, as this makes it much easier for our volunteers to make sure the right customers receive your goods in the right quantities.

All goods need to be dropped off at The Woods by 3:00 p.m. on Delivery Day. You can find out when the next Delivery day is by checking the calendar on our wesite.

If you wish to make other arrangements to deliver your goods please contact us and we'll help you organise transport of your goods.

If you are unsatisfied with the quality of a purchase please contact us right away. We will contact the producer and arrange for you to receive a complete refund for that product you purchased.
