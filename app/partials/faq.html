You can sign up as a Producer or Customer member here. You will then be required to pay the invoiced amount.

Please note that if you want to become a Producer Member, you will need to fill out a specialised application form, which will be reviewed before your membership can be approved. However, anyone can sign up as a Customer member.

Becoming a Producer Member allows you to use our website to sell your products. The slightly higher cost allows us to continue maintaining the website for you, and pays for reveiwing and approving Producer applications.

Most producers recoup the cost of membership quickly by selling their products through the NNFC. However, should a producer wish to leave the co-op, the membership fee is completely refundable.

You can upload products any time. When you upload a product you pick which Delivery Days to sell that product for. Then your product will appear in the store during the shopping time for that delivery day.

For example, if you regularly sell a product, you can upload it once and set it to sell repeatedly for the dates you choose. Alternatively if you expect your animals to be butchered and ready for sale by a certain date, you can upload them significantly in advance.

Should you need to make changes to your products, you can make changes on a cycle-per-cycle basis. For example if you regularly sell apples in autumn but then unexpectedly have less available midway through the season, you can edit the apples in the store to reduce the quantity available for each delivery day to represent your reduced crop.

In most cases, if you grew it, made it, or raised it in Northland, you can sell it. However, there are a few exceptions.

We are proud to be GE free. Genetically engineered products will not be accepted for sale. This extends to goods that have GE ingredients, and animal products where the animal was fed genetically engineered material. If you'd like to transition your operation away from GE inputs, please contact us and we can give you free consulting. As a Natural Food Co-op, we also require that producers declare all chemicals used in their production process. You can still sell your goods through the NNFC, but all chemical agents must be declared.

Finally, for the sake of the animals, we do not accept goods derived from Confined Animal Feeding Operations (such as battery hens). Please keep your livestock free range.

Check the calendar on our website to find out what days you can shop. All memebers will receive emails notifying them that ordering is now open.

If you don't see many products in the store, check back later as producers may not have uploaded all they plan to sell yet.

Why only have a set time?

A set time for shopping is used because the co-op is technically not a store. We have no inventory and no way to ship orders on our own. We do show you what other producers are selling and facilitate a convenient and efficient distribution system via volunteer sorters and drivers.

Check the calendar on our website to find out when you must have paid your shopping invoice to us. Ideally we like to have received payment before delivery day so we can pay the producers after they've delivered member's orders.

Raw Milk can be added to your cart just like any other product in the store. However, raw milk is delivered fresh to our sorting center (1 Woods Rd, Whangarei) every Tuesday.

You pay for your milk at the same time as any other products you've ordered as normal.

When you order Raw Milk in our store, you are ordering milk for two weeks. Your order is prepaid and the quantity you order is how much you will receive over two weeks.

Each week you will receive 1/2 the full amount paid for.

Pick up time for Raw Milk is 3-6pm every Tuesday.

Member's orders are collected and sorted on Delivery Day. Delivery day is usually the second and last Tuesday's of each month.

If you live in Whangarei, or want to collect your food while you’re in town, you can pick up your order from The Woods (1 Woods Road), in central Whangarei. We are behind Chipmunks. Orders are avaible for collection from 3:00 p.m. onwards on Delivery Day. You can find out when the next Delivery Day is by checking the calendar on our wesite.

If Whangarei is too far for you to travel, you can pick up your goods from a collection point closer to home. To find out times and places for community collection points, you’ll need to join a delivery route. Please also get in touch with your local Route Manager. Contact details for our Route Managers in communities all over Northland can be found here.

If there is no delivery route to your area, please feel free to shop anyway and the co-op will arrange your delivery.
